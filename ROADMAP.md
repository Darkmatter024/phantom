# PHANTOM — Capability & Gap Roadmap

**Lens (locked): MULTI-DCT** — optimizing to hand PHANTOM to other techs (portability, guardrails, onboarding, "would a tech who didn’t build this understand it?").
**Type:** outward-looking strategy review (not a code audit). **Baseline:** v1.14.73, audit campaign complete. **Date:** 2026-06-25.
**Method:** 9-agent review (5 pillar-depth × field-gaps × hardening × AUS-01 pressure-test → leverage-ranked synthesis). **LOCAL-ONLY — do not push.**

---

## Verdict

PHANTOM is field-ready as a SOLO tool and shallow-to-broken as a MULTI-DCT tool. The deploy engine, audit trail, handoff HTML export, Optic Score Verify (EDP check), and the first-run onboarding gate are genuinely hand-off-grade. But the locked lens (hand to a tech who did NOT build this; portability is load-bearing) exposes one systemic failure that recurs across every probe: the data a second tech most needs cannot MOVE. Capture works everywhere; local retrieve works everywhere; MOVE-between-techs is broken for the three crown-jewel corpora — the Ghost Echo QUIRK store (tribal knowledge, no export/import at all, verified: GHOST_ECHO_KEY only read/written locally at L21004/21008/21021, receivedVia is a dead Phase-2 stub at L21002/21085), the Master source-of-truth (single key, replace destroys prior site at L25454; omitted from the backup bundle L39924-39943), and the Master-derived scope snapshot (also omitted). On top of portability, the second systemic gap is SILENT DEGRADATION dressed as success: a tech sees a clean reconcile checkmark on a cab-only restored Master, a green DOM verdict computed from hardcoded universal thresholds (L34065, divorced from the per-optic rxMin/alarmLow that DO exist in OPTIC_DB), and a confident 'no history' from a matcher keyed on free-text rackId — every one of these lies to a tech who can't tell. Third, the offline contract leaks: every AI surface dead-ends with no navigator.onLine pre-check (phantomAI L14696; retry budget L14590 delays [8000,15000]/maxRetries 2 = ~23s of spinner) and the SCAN rack classifier is hardcoded to L-NN/R-NN (L42320 RACK_REGEX /^[LR]-\d{2}$/i), so any site not using the author's naming silently mis-tags racks as ASSET. The roadmap below is ordered by leverage = field-impact / build-cost, and it deliberately front-loads the cheap-but-load-bearing portability and trust fixes over net-new pillar surface, because a second tech hitting any one of the silent-lie paths loses trust in the whole tool and falls back to a laptop.

## AUS-01 site-profile verdict (the pre-decided question)

Building out the AUS-01 site profile does NOT outrank the P0 work — it is downstream of it, and on its own it is a near-zero-value move under the locked lens. Here's the field-grounded reason: a richly-filled AUS-01 profile is just more data trapped on one device, and worse, it actively makes things worse today because the quirk-retrieval gate hard-filters on q.siteId === currentSite (L21112) and the Master is single-key — so a detailed AUS-01 profile means a tech who switches to or imports from DFW sees NOTHING, and a second tech whose profile isn't byte-identical to AUS-01 has their imported quirks silently suppressed. You'd be sharpening the exact knife that cuts the multi-DCT use case. The correct sequencing and scope: (1) first ship quirk export/import + the tolerant site-scope match (P0#1 + P1#1) so site profiles can differ without silently nuking data; (2) then, when you DO build AUS-01, scope it as a TEMPLATE/exemplar profile — the canonical, exportable 'this is what a complete site profile looks like, copy and edit for your site' — not a one-off hardcode for the author's home site. That template doubles as onboarding (an empty-state a new tech fills against a real example) and as the source of the rack-ID naming pattern that fixes the SCAN classifier (P1#3) and the IndexedDB rackId canonicalization (P2). So: AUS-01 profile = valuable, but only as a portable template AFTER the portability and tolerant-matching plumbing exists. As the literal next move it's a trap; as the demonstration payload that proves the P0 portability work end-to-end, it's the right thing to build third or fourth, not first.

## Next 1–3 (with "why this before that")

1. **Ghost Echo quirk export/import + add phantom_ghost_echo_v1 to the backup bundle (P0 #1)**
   - It is the single load-bearing portability gap with the lowest build cost — the irreplaceable tribal knowledge already captures and retrieves locally, only MOVE is missing, so one small mirror of the existing export path unlocks the whole multi-DCT premise. Everything else assumes data can reach a second tech; this is the cheapest way to make that true.
2. **Tolerant site-scope match for quirk retrieval (P1 #1)**
   - Ship it in the same stroke as the export, because the moment quirks start arriving from another device the byte-exact q.siteId gate (L21112) will silently swallow them — the export would land and show nothing, reading as 'broken' to exactly the new tech we're building for. The fix is small and prevents the export from looking dead on arrival.
3. **Wire renderDOM/Verify to OPTIC_DB per-optic thresholds + gloved numeric Rx entry (P0 #3)**
   - This is the highest-stakes silent lie that's pure wiring, not new data — the per-optic thresholds already exist on every OPTIC_DB entry, so this converts a verdict that false-alarms every long-reach link (and is unreachable gloved) into the trustworthy bad-reading call techs reach for. It precedes the Master/AI hardening because a wrong green light on a fiber reading is the fastest way to lose a second tech's trust in the tool.

## Not now (explicit deferrals)

- **Site-keyed multi-master store + in-app site switcher (the 'true' multi-site Master architecture)** — L-effort architectural rewrite of every Master read/write path plus data migration. The P0 Export/Import-Master bridge solves the actual immediate pain (hand-off + rotating between sites) at a fraction of the cost. Promote only once daily two-site-on-one-device coexistence is a proven recurring need, not a hypothetical.
- **Net-new pillar surface: per-port deployment-rack cabling, cross-rack/cross-site asset lookup, fiber-plant/polarity/MPO per-link checks** — All real depth gaps, but each is 'PHANTOM can't do X yet' (a limit a tech understands) rather than 'PHANTOM told me X and was wrong' (a trust-breaker). The whole roadmap deliberately fixes silent lies and portability before adding surface, because new features built on an untrustworthy or non-portable base inherit those flaws.
- **LEGEND/SITE-VARS sheet parsing into the Profile tab** — Good ingest depth and very multi-DCT-aligned, but it's net-new parser work to surface data that the author can currently hand-fill, whereas the P0 items wire up load-bearing data that already exists and cannot move. Lower leverage now; revisit after portability is solved.
- **PENDING-echo resolution affordance and IndexedDB rackId canonicalization** — Both are slow corpus-quality leaks, not aisle fallback moments. The rackId fix in particular should wait until naming is centralized via the P1 site-profile/Master work so there's a canonical form to normalize against — doing it first would just hardcode another convention.

GHOST ECHO is actually TWO unrelated systems wearing one name, and the split is the dominant multi-DCT gap. The "Phase 2 QR sync" promised in the L20988 comment is vaporware. ### pillar-depth: fiber-optics

The FIBER+OPTICS pillar has a deep, genuinely useful REFERENCE layer (18 platforms in OPTIC_DB with optic/PN/fiber/connector/DOM/Rx-range/vendor-lock, plus rich static troubleshooting cards) and a real offline Verify state-machine that scans switch->port->optic and logs an audited verdict with override reasons. That is strong, hand-off-friendly groundwork. But the pillar does NOT finish the job on the single most field-critical task: VERIFYING A READING. The per-optic rxMin/rxMax/alarmLow values are present on every entry in OPTIC_DB and are DISPLAYED as reference text, yet NO code path ever compares a tech's actual Rx/DOM reading against the specific optic's thresholds. The only reading-evaluation code (renderDOM, line 34065) uses HARDCODED universal thresholds (v<-11 alarm, v<-9 marginal, v>4 high) that are divorced from OPTIC_DB and produce wrong verdicts for ER4/long-reach optics. Worse, that path requires PASTING raw CLI text — impossible with gloved hands on an iPad at the cold aisle; it presumes a laptop/SSH session. So in the aisle the verdict 'is this Rx good for THIS optic?' is a dead-end: the data to answer it exists but is never wired to an input. Verify mode also rests on deploy_inferOptic (line 22352), a 2-branch SMF/MMF guess that emits generic strings like 'QSFP-DD-DR4' / 'OSFP-800G-NDR' regardless of the actual platform's qualified SKU, so the 'Expected' optic a handed-off tech sees can be wrong while looking authoritative. Under multi-DCT, the reference layer is usable cold by a tech with no tribal knowledge; the verify/verdict layer quietly degrades and the bad-optic / bad-reading judgment that techs actually reach for is the gap.

### Field-hardening

PHANTOM has a genuinely strong hardening spine for a single-file offline monolith: safeStore() centralizes quota handling with a loud user-visible toast and a window.__phantomStorageFull flag (dct-ios.html:14205), the storage gas-gauge fires throttled 85/95% warnings (40180+), a global crash logger persists JS errors and surfaces a tap-to-view banner (14507), the AI proxy path has real retry/timeout/diagnostic classification (14584-14676), AI-dependent surfaces are CSS-gated by body[data-net] when offline (40160-40172), and phantomImport() does an atomic snapshot-rollback restore with a pre-flight headroom check and referential-integrity filtering (40419-40463) — that restore path is the single most field-hardened piece of code in the file. The gaps that block handing this to another tech are about DATA YOU CANNOT GET BACK, not crashes. The headline is a data-portability hole: the Discrepancy List (phantom_discrepancies_v1) — which embeds base64 field photos and is the audit trail of what was wrong in the aisle — is NOT included in exportAllData()/phantomImport(), so the one dataset a relieving tech most needs is silently dropped by the backup. Combined with IndexedDB import paths that hard-code open('GhostEchoDB', 1) with no version-mismatch handling, an unbounded base64-photo growth path with no per-deployment budget, and a crash log that can only be read via a 1500-char-truncated confirm() dialog, these are the things that turn "works on my bench" into "I can't trust the handoff." Under MULTI-DCT the export/import omission is the most serious: a tech who did NOT build this will export a backup, hand it off or restore it on a fresh iPad, and lose every discrepancy + photo without any warning that data was left behind.
